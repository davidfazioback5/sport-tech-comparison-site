Platform Summaries

360Player

Editor's Pick for Club Management
9.2 / 10

360Player offers the most complete club management suite of any platform we tested, with particularly strong tools for player development tracking alongside standard admin features. It's built with professional and semi-professional clubs in mind, offering white-label apps, deep reporting, and API integrations. The learning curve is steeper than grassroots tools, and pricing reflects its pro-focused feature set.

Best for: Academies, semi-pro clubs, and organisations that need performance data and admin under one roof.

Spond

8.8 / 10

Spond's free tier makes it exceptional value for grassroots and amateur clubs. Its availability management and event tools are genuinely best-in-class at the community level. It falls short on monetisation, reporting depth, and integrations — but for clubs that just need reliable admin without a big budget, it's hard to argue against.

Best for: Grassroots clubs, amateur leagues, small volunteer-run organisations.

Full Review

TeamSnap

8.5 / 10

TeamSnap is a mature, well-rounded platform with a large user base — particularly in North America. It covers scheduling, rostering, payments, and communication effectively. European clubs may find some features and payment integrations less tailored to their market.

Best for: US and Canadian youth leagues, recreational clubs with multi-team structures.

Full Review

Revolutionise Sport

Best for AU/NZ
9.1 / 10

Revolutionise Sport is the dominant club management platform in Australia and New Zealand — purpose-built for the local market with local compliance, payment gateways, and support. It excels at registration, competition management, and scaling across association structures. The interface can feel dated but the depth is unmatched for ANZ clubs.

Best for: Australian and New Zealand clubs, associations, and national sporting bodies.

Full Review + Demo →

TidyHQ

Best Value Grassroots
8.2 / 10

TidyHQ is the easiest-to-use club admin tool we've tested — exceptional for volunteer-run clubs that need memberships, meeting minutes, event management, and basic finances in one affordable package. It doesn't scale to large clubs or associations, but at its target market it's hard to beat.

Best for: Grassroots clubs, community organisations, and volunteer committees.

Full Review →

TeamStack

7.8 / 10

TeamStack solves a specific and common problem: rostering complexity. It's the strongest platform for clubs managing multiple teams, volunteer coordinators, and venues simultaneously. It works best as a complement to a fuller platform rather than a standalone club management solution.

Best for: Multi-team clubs with complex rostering and scheduling needs.

Full Review

Dribl

Best AU Competition
7.5 / 10

Dribl is rapidly becoming the go-to competition management platform for Australian football associations. Its fixture management, live scoring, and digital match reports are best-in-class. It's accessed through associations rather than directly by clubs, and pairs well with Revolutionise or TidyHQ for club-level admin.

Best for: Football associations and leagues running structured competitions.

Scoring Methodology

Our club management score is weighted across 12 sub-criteria: member registration (15%), scheduling (15%), communication tools (12%), payment processing (12%), reporting (10%), mobile app quality (10%), onboarding ease (8%), multi-team support (8%), integrations (5%), support quality (3%), security/compliance (2%).

Each platform was evaluated through a 30-day hands-on trial, vendor briefings, and structured testing by our editorial team. Scores are updated at least twice per year.
