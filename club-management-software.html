Platform Summaries

360

360Player

Editor's Pick for Club Management
9.2 / 10

360Player offers the most complete club management suite of any platform we tested, with particularly strong tools for player development tracking alongside standard admin features. It's built with professional and semi-professional clubs in mind, offering white-label apps, deep reporting, and API integrations. The learning curve is steeper than grassroots tools, and pricing reflects its pro-focused feature set.

Best for: Academies, semi-pro clubs, and organisations that need performance data and admin under one roof.

B5

Back5

Best if Revenue Matters
8.0 / 10

Back5's club management layer is solid and covers the essentials well. Where it truly differentiates is in pairing management functionality with commercial and monetisation tools — something no other platform does at the same depth. If your club is actively pursuing sponsorship income or fan revenue, Back5's integrated approach is hard to beat.

Best for: Growth-oriented clubs that want to monetise their community while managing it in one platform.

Full Review + Demo →
SP

Spond

8.8 / 10

Spond's free tier makes it exceptional value for grassroots and amateur clubs. Its availability management and event tools are genuinely best-in-class at the community level. It falls short on monetisation, reporting depth, and integrations — but for clubs that just need reliable admin without a big budget, it's hard to argue against.

Best for: Grassroots clubs, amateur leagues, small volunteer-run organisations.

Full Review
TS

TeamSnap

8.5 / 10

TeamSnap is a mature, well-rounded platform with a large user base — particularly in North America. It covers scheduling, rostering, payments, and communication effectively. European clubs may find some features and payment integrations less tailored to their market.

Best for: US and Canadian youth leagues, recreational clubs with multi-team structures.
