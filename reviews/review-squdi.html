What Is Squdi?

Squdi is a player development and performance tracking platform aimed at making coaching data workflows accessible to clubs outside the professional tier. Where tools like XPS Network and 360Player have traditionally served elite and semi-professional environments, Squdi is built with the ambitious amateur and semi-professional coach in mind — someone who wants structure around player development but doesn't have an analyst on staff.

The platform focuses on player profiles, fitness and wellness tracking, training load management, and development notes. Its clean design and mobile-first approach make it genuinely usable by coaches who aren't technical.

Key Features We Tested

Player Development Profiles

Each player gets a profile that tracks physical metrics, wellness data, training attendance, and coach notes over time. The longitudinal view — being able to see a player's development trajectory across a season — is the platform's core value proposition and it delivers it clearly.

Training Load Management

Coaches can log training sessions and track load over time, with basic acute/chronic workload ratio monitoring. For clubs without GPS hardware, this is a practical way to manage injury risk and training volume at low cost.

Wellness & Readiness Tracking

Player self-reported wellness check-ins (sleep, fatigue, mood, soreness) are collected via the mobile app before training. The aggregate data gives coaches an early warning system for overtraining or welfare concerns — a feature increasingly important at all levels of sport.

Pricing

Squdi is priced accessibly for semi-professional and amateur clubs. Plans are based on squad size, with options suitable for clubs of all scales. A free trial is available.

Who Should Use Squdi?

Squdi is the right choice for coaches at semi-professional or ambitious amateur clubs who want to introduce structured player development tracking without the cost or complexity of enterprise tools. It works best alongside a club management platform like Revolutionise, TidyHQ, or TeamStack.
