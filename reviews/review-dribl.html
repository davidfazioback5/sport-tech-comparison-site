What Is Dribl?

Dribl is an Australian sports technology company that has built a modern, mobile-first competition management platform primarily for football (soccer) associations and leagues. It handles the end-to-end competition workflow: creating draws and fixtures, assigning referees, recording results, updating ladders in real time, and publishing everything publicly so that players, parents, and fans can follow their competition easily.

The platform has grown rapidly through Australian state football federations and regional associations, replacing legacy competition management tools with a far more modern experience. Its public-facing competition pages — with live scores, standings, and match histories — are significantly better designed than most alternatives.

Key Features We Tested

Fixture Management & Draw Generation

Dribl's draw generation and fixture scheduling tools are excellent. Competition administrators can create rounds, assign venues, manage bye rounds, and handle finals structures with minimal manual work. Conflict detection for venue clashes and team scheduling gaps is built in.

Live Scores & Match Updates

The match day experience is a standout. Referees or club administrators can enter live scores via the mobile app, and updates are reflected immediately in the public ladder. For associations running multiple concurrent games, this real-time visibility is enormously valuable for participants.

Digital Match Reports

Match reports, goal scorers, yellow and red cards, and other match data are recorded digitally and stored permanently. This replaces paper-based processes and gives associations a searchable record of all match incidents — useful for appeals and disciplinary processes.

Referee Management

Referee assignment, availability management, and payment tracking are supported within the platform. For associations managing large referee pools across many games each weekend, this is a meaningful time-saver.

Pricing

Dribl is priced per association or competition, with plans based on the number of teams and competitions managed. Individual club access is typically provided through their association. Pricing is competitive with legacy competition management systems and significantly better value given the modern feature set.

Who Should Use Dribl?

Dribl is primarily for football associations and leagues running structured competitions — junior, senior, and futsal. Clubs that access it do so through their governing association. For club-level management tools (membership, training, communications), Dribl should be paired with Revolutionise, TidyHQ, or Spond.
