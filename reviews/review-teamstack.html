What Is TeamStack?

TeamStack is a cloud-based team management and rostering platform that started life as a workforce scheduling tool and has been adapted for sports club use. It's particularly strong for clubs that run multiple teams, manage volunteer coaches and referees, or need to coordinate resources across several venues simultaneously.

The platform is used across Australia, New Zealand, and the UK for amateur and semi-professional sports clubs, as well as community organisations managing volunteer rotas. Its strength — and its limitation — is that it does rostering and scheduling extremely well and not much else.

Key Features We Tested

Rostering & Scheduling

This is where TeamStack shines. The drag-and-drop schedule builder, combined with real-time availability tracking, makes building weekly rosters genuinely fast. The system flags conflicts automatically — double-booked coaches, unavailable players, venue clashes — and the mobile app means participants can update their availability from anywhere.

Communication Tools

Push notifications, in-app messaging, and automated reminders are well-implemented. Players get notified about training sessions, game times, and schedule changes without the admin needing to manually contact each person.

Multi-team Structure

TeamStack handles multi-team clubs cleanly. Administrators get a top-level view across all teams, while coaches only see their own squads. This separation of concerns is well-thought-out and reduces the admin overhead for clubs running many age groups.

Pricing

TeamStack offers tiered plans based on the number of users and locations. A free tier exists for very small teams, with paid plans scaling up for larger clubs. It's competitively priced for what it does.

Who Should Use TeamStack?

TeamStack suits clubs whose primary pain point is rostering complexity — managing many teams, volunteer coordinators, and venues. It works well as a complement to a fuller platform like Revolutionise or TidyHQ. Clubs looking for an all-in-one solution should look at 360Player or Revolutionise Sport instead.
