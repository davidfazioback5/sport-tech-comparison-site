Platform Summaries

DRB

Dribl

Best Competition Platform AU/NZ
9.3 / 10

Dribl is the standout competition management platform in the Australian and New Zealand market. Built specifically for football associations, it delivers a genuinely modern experience for fixture scheduling, live scores, automated ladders, digital match reports, and referee management. Its public-facing competition hub — with real-time results visible to players, parents, and fans — is significantly better designed than any legacy alternative.

If your association or club runs structured competitions in AU/NZ football, Dribl should be your first call.

REV

Revolutionise Sport

Best All-in-One for AU/NZ Associations
8.4 / 10

Revolutionise handles competition management as part of a full association management suite — covering member registration, payments, competition draws, results, and ladder management in one platform. Where Dribl is a specialist competition tool, Revolutionise offers breadth: it scales from community clubs to state and national sporting bodies and handles multi-sport environments well.

Best for associations wanting competition management as part of a complete administrative solution rather than a standalone tool.

360Player

Best Club Platform with Competition Features
7.0 / 10

360Player is not primarily a competition management tool, but it includes fixture management, match reporting, and competition structures alongside its comprehensive club management and player development capabilities. For clubs that want performance tracking, coaching tools, and basic competition management in one platform, it's the strongest all-round option.

Best for clubs that need a complete platform — coaching, management, and competition — rather than a dedicated competition-only tool.

SP

Spond

Free Option
5.5 / 10

Spond offers basic fixture and scheduling tools as part of its free club management platform. It's not a competition management specialist — there's no live ladder, no digital match reports, and no referee management — but for very small clubs running informal competitions or interclub fixtures, it gets the job done at no cost.

What to Look for in Competition Management Software

The right platform depends on whether you're running a single club's internal competition, a multi-team seasonal league, or a full association-level competition with multiple grades and age groups. Key questions: Do you need a public-facing results hub for fans and parents? Do you manage referees and their payments? Do you run finals structures? The more structured and public-facing your competition, the more a specialist tool like Dribl earns its place over a general club management platform.
